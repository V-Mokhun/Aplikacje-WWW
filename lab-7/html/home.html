Top 5 Biggest Bridges in the World

1. Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge

The Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge is the world's longest bridge, spanning an impressive 164.8 kilometers (102.4 miles). Located in China, it's part of the Beijing-Shanghai high-speed railway line. This viaduct bridge was completed in 2011 and crosses lowland rice paddies, canals, rivers, and lakes.

2. Changhua–Kaohsiung Viaduct

The Changhua–Kaohsiung Viaduct in Taiwan is the second-longest bridge in the world, measuring 157.3 kilometers (97.8 miles). It's part of the Taiwan High Speed Rail network and was opened in 2007. This continuous viaduct helps to minimize the impact on the ground-level farmland it passes over.

3. Tianjin Grand Bridge

The Tianjin Grand Bridge in China is the third-longest bridge globally, with a length of 113.7 kilometers (70.6 miles). It's part of the Beijing-Shanghai high-speed railway line and was completed in 2010. This viaduct bridge crosses the North China Plain, spanning both urban and rural areas.

4. Weinan Weihe Grand Bridge

The Weinan Weihe Grand Bridge is another impressive Chinese bridge, measuring 79.7 kilometers (49.5 miles). Completed in 2008, it's part of the Zhengzhou–Xi'an high-speed railway line. The bridge crosses the Wei River twice and spans the flood plains of the river.

5. Bang Na Expressway

The Bang Na Expressway in Thailand, also known as the Burapha Withi Expressway, is 54 kilometers (33.5 miles) long. While technically an elevated highway, it's often considered one of the world's longest bridges. Completed in 2000, it connects Bangkok to the Bang Na-Trat Highway, significantly reducing travel time in the area.
